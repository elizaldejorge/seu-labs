Guidelines

Build with purpose, safety, and sustainability.

SEU Labs projects should solve a real academic problem, avoid unnecessary risk, and have a realistic plan for support after the prototype stage.

✓

Good fit

Projects should be useful for teaching, learning, academic work, or repeatable university processes.

⚑

Clear audience

The intended users should be defined before technical decisions are made.

↗

Scalable idea

Strong projects can become examples, templates, or models for future faculty innovation.

Security and privacy

Review the risk before building.

Every project should be reviewed for what data it uses, where the data is stored, who can access it, and whether it connects to university systems.

  • Avoid collecting sensitive data unless it is necessary and approved.
  • Do not expose protected information through AI tools or public apps.
  • Plan how user accounts, access, authorization, and permissions will work.
  • Identify whether the app uses AI only for coding or inside the final product.
  • Estimate hosting, API, storage, bandwidth, and token costs before launch.

Review criteria

What the review should answer.

Value

Does the idea solve a meaningful problem, improve a process, or support teaching and learning?

Feasibility

Can the project be prototyped with the available tools, budget, timeline, and support?

Data and compliance

Does the project involve student information, PII, FERPA-protected data, or other sensitive records?

Lifecycle

Who owns the project, how will it be maintained, and what happens after the pilot period?
