What is SEU Labs?

A secure sandbox for responsible AI innovation.

SEU Labs is designed for faculty who want to create AI-assisted or “vibe-coded” applications using tools such as Gemini, GitHub, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, Replit, and Google-based development options.

01

Encourage innovation

Support creative teaching and learning ideas without leaving faculty to build alone.

02

Reduce shadow IT

Create a visible, reviewed process for academic apps before they become risk points.

03

Protect university data

Review privacy, FERPA, data use, account access, and integration concerns early.

How the process works

Clear steps from proposal to launch.

Each project moves through intake, review, development, testing, and lifecycle planning.

1

Submit

Faculty submit a Google Form with the project idea, goals, audience, and technical background.

2

Intake meeting

The team reviews usefulness, feasibility, data needs, AI usage, integrations, and support needs.

3

Internal profile

Staff create a project profile with risks, estimated costs, recommended path, and next steps.

4

Review

Security, privacy, FERPA, infrastructure, user accounts, and scaling risks are reviewed.

5

Build

Approved projects receive a designated technical path and support from staff and student ambassadors.

6

Test and decide

The prototype is tested, documented, launched, revised, continued, or retired.

Project guidelines

What makes a strong SEU Labs project?

Good projects solve a real problem, fit the pilot, and can be reviewed before deployment.

Educational value

The project should support teaching, learning, academic work, or a clear university need.

Feasibility

The project should be realistic to prototype and should not duplicate an existing SEU tool unnecessarily.

Responsible data use

The project must avoid unnecessary PII and follow FERPA, AI policy, and security expectations.

Questions reviewed before approval

Does the project collect data?

Where is the data stored?

Does it use FERPA-protected information?

Does it require user accounts?

Does it connect to Google Drive or SEU systems?

Where is AI used?

What are the hosting or token costs?

Who maintains it after the pilot year?
