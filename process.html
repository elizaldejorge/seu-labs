Process

A clear path from idea to launch.

The process is designed to move quickly without skipping the reviews that protect students, faculty, data, and university systems.

01

Submit a proposal

Faculty submit a short intake form describing the idea, audience, workflow, data needs, and comfort level with AI-assisted coding tools.

02

Intake conversation

The project is discussed with the SEU Labs team to clarify the problem, understand the intended user experience, and identify early risks.

03

Project profile

A concise project profile is created with the purpose, audience, data use, AI use, integrations, cost considerations, and recommended technical path.

04

Review

The project is reviewed for value, feasibility, security, privacy, FERPA concerns, integration needs, hosting needs, and long-term maintenance.

05

Prototype

Approved projects receive a suggested stack and development support. The goal is a working prototype that can be tested with a small audience.

06

Test and decide

The prototype is tested, documented, and reviewed. It may be launched, revised, continued, paused, migrated, or retired depending on pilot criteria.
